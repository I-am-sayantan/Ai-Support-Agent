EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK 2025-2026

=== WELCOME ===

Welcome to the team! This handbook provides essential information about company culture, policies, benefits, and expectations. Please read it thoroughly and refer back as needed.

=== COMPANY VALUES ===

Innovation: We embrace new ideas and encourage creative problem-solving
Integrity: We act ethically and transparently in all situations
Collaboration: We work together and value diverse perspectives
Excellence: We strive for high quality in everything we do
Customer Focus: We put our customers at the center of all decisions

=== COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS ===

Salary:
- Paid bi-weekly via direct deposit
- Annual performance reviews in Q1
- Merit increases based on performance (average 3-8%)
- Promotion salary adjustments (10-15% typical)
- Cost of living adjustments reviewed annually

Health Insurance:
- Medical, dental, and vision coverage
- Company pays 80% of employee premiums
- Coverage begins on first day of employment
- Dependent coverage available (employee pays 50%)
- HSA available with high-deductible plan option
- Annual enrollment period in November

Retirement:
- 401(k) plan with immediate enrollment
- Company matches 50% up to 6% of salary
- Vesting schedule: 20% per year, fully vested at 5 years
- Financial advisor services available quarterly
- Automatic enrollment at 3% (can opt out or adjust)

Equity:
- Stock options for all employees hired before 2024
- RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) for employees hired 2024+
- Vesting: 4-year schedule with 1-year cliff
- Grant amounts based on level and performance
- Refresh grants reviewed annually

Bonuses:
- Performance bonus: 0-20% of base salary
- Paid in Q1 based on prior year performance
- Company performance multiplier (0.5x - 2x)
- Department goals factor into calculations
- Sign-on bonuses for select roles (1-2 year clawback)

Other Benefits:
- Life insurance: 2x annual salary (company-paid)
- Disability insurance (short and long-term)
- Commuter benefits: Pre-tax transit/parking ($300/month max)
- Gym membership reimbursement: $50/month
- Professional development: $2,000/year per employee
- Tuition assistance: $5,000/year for degree programs
- Employee assistance program (EAP): Free confidential counseling

=== WORKING HOURS AND ATTENDANCE ===

Standard Hours:
- Full-time: 40 hours per week
- Core hours: 10 AM - 3 PM (must be available)
- Flexible start time: 7 AM - 10 AM
- Lunch break: 30-60 minutes (unpaid)
- Department-specific schedules may vary

Overtime:
- Non-exempt employees: 1.5x pay over 40 hours/week
- Overtime must be pre-approved by manager
- Comp time not available (cash payment only)
- Weekend work: Prior approval required
- Holiday work: 2x regular pay

Attendance:
- Punctuality expected for all meetings
- Notify manager of absences before start of shift
- Excessive tardiness subject to disciplinary action
- Three unexcused absences may result in termination
- Doctor's notes required for absences over 3 days

Remote Work:
- See Remote Work Policy document
- Up to 3 days per week for eligible employees
- Must maintain productivity standards
- Equipment provided by company
- Regular check-ins with manager required

=== PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT ===

Training Opportunities:
- New hire orientation (2-day program)
- Role-specific training in first 90 days
- Quarterly lunch-and-learn sessions
- Annual conferences (manager approval required)
- Online learning platforms (LinkedIn Learning, Udemy)
- Mentorship program (optional, highly encouraged)

Career Growth:
- Annual performance reviews with development plan
- Internal job postings sent weekly
- Employees encouraged to apply for internal roles
- Promotion cycles: January and July
- Leadership development program for managers
- Individual contributor track available (no management required)

Certifications:
- Company pays for job-relevant certifications
- Study time allowed during work hours (reasonable amounts)
- Bonus for achieving relevant certifications ($500-$2,500)
- Recertification costs covered
- Examples: PMP, AWS, Salesforce, CPA, etc.

=== WORKPLACE POLICIES ===

Dress Code:
- Business casual Monday-Thursday
- Casual Friday (jeans acceptable)
- Client meetings: Business professional
- No offensive graphics or messages on clothing
- Safety gear required in lab/warehouse areas

Technology Use:
- Company devices for work purposes only
- Personal use permitted during breaks
- No illegal downloads or streaming
- Social media use should not interfere with work
- IT monitors network for security (minimal privacy expectation)
- Personal devices must be registered for network access

Confidentiality:
- Sign NDA on first day of employment
- Do not discuss confidential information outside company
- Secure all documents when leaving workspace
- Do not share passwords
- Report data breaches immediately
- Return all company property upon termination

Safety:
- Report hazards to facilities immediately
- First aid kits located on each floor
- Emergency exits clearly marked
- Fire drills conducted quarterly
- Ergonomic assessments available upon request
- Violence or threats: Zero tolerance, immediate termination

=== PERFORMANCE AND DISCIPLINE ===

Performance Reviews:
- Annual reviews in January
- Mid-year check-ins in July
- 360-degree feedback collected from peers
- Self-assessment required
- Goals set quarterly using OKR framework
- Ratings: Exceeds, Meets, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory

Performance Improvement Plans (PIP):
- Implemented for "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory" ratings
- Duration: 60-90 days
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- Specific, measurable goals defined
- Successful completion removes from record
- Failure to improve may result in termination

Disciplinary Action:
Progressive discipline policy:
1. Verbal warning (documented)
2. Written warning
3. Final written warning
4. Termination

Immediate termination offenses:
- Theft, fraud, or embezzlement
- Violence or threats of violence
- Harassment or discrimination
- Gross insubordination
- Breach of confidentiality
- Intoxication at work
- Falsification of records

=== SEPARATION ===

Resignation:
- Two weeks notice requested (not required)
- Exit interview conducted by HR
- Return all company property
- Final paycheck includes unused vacation
- COBRA information provided for benefits continuation
- Reference policy: Dates of employment and title only

Termination:
- May be with or without cause
- Severance based on tenure and level (typically 2-4 weeks)
- Sign release of claims to receive severance
- Non-compete enforced (if applicable)
- Unemployment eligibility varies by circumstances

Retirement:
- Notify HR at least 3 months in advance
- Retirement party arranged by team
- 401(k) rollover assistance provided
- Retiree status: Discount on company products
- Alumni network access
